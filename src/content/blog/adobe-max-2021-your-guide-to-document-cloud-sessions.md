It is that time again for Adobe MAX — The Creativity Conference. Adobe MAX is a uniquely immersive and engaging digital experience. This year, it is a free and virtual event and will feature live and on-demand content, including more than 400+ sessions, labs, keynotes, musical performances, special guest appearances, and a sneak peek at things being worked on in Adobe labs.

With so many fantastic sessions, we wanted to take a moment and provide you a guide to catch some of the wonderful sessions related to Adobe Document Cloud and developers. These sessions are all great ways to learn more how you can get the best out of automating and digitizing your document experiences.

## Sessions

### Creatives Are Not Robots: Letting Automation Do That for You

![](/blog/adobe-max-2021-your-guide-to-document-cloud-sessions/02.jpeg)

Session ID: S203Times: Tues, Oct 27th 11:30 AM — 12:00 PM PSTThu, Oct 28th 6:00 AM — 6:30 AM PST

In this session, Ben Vanderberg, Principal Developer Evangelist for Adobe, will showcase how using Adobe Creative Cloud APIs and Adobe Document Services APIs can be used to streamline creative tasks to free up creatives to do what they do best: be creative. Some of the solutions will feature Photoshop API, PDF Embed API, Document Generation API, and PDF Services API. Come and join live to ask your questions.

### Hidden Gems of Acrobat for Education

![](/blog/adobe-max-2021-your-guide-to-document-cloud-sessions/03.png)

Session ID: S280Times: Thu, Oct 28th, 3:30 PM — 4:00 PM PST

Lori DeFurio, Principal Evangelist at Adobe, will help you learn about powerful features of Adobe Acrobat that educators, students, and staff can use to go digital, speed up processes, and create a better learning environment. The tips and tricks will have you saying, “I didn’t know Acrobat could do that!”

In this session, you’ll learn how to:

- Get work done quickly and efficiently with the top 5 Document Cloud tools
- Work with PDFs directly in Microsoft 365 and Google Drive
- Create fillable, interactive PDFs to collect assignments and information
- Grade papers and reports digitally and return them to students
- Use Liquid Mode for easier reading on your smartphone or tablet

### Learning the Fundamentals of PDF Accessibility Step by Step

![](/blog/adobe-max-2021-your-guide-to-document-cloud-sessions/04.png)

Session ID: L200Times: Wed, Oct 27th, 6:30 AM PST — 7:30 AM PST Wed, Oct 27th, 1:00 PM PST — 2:00PM PST

Document accessibility begins with a good foundation. Join Accessible Document Specialist Dax Castro in this follow-along lab as he unravels PDF accessibility step by step. His clear approach to accessibility basics, from accessibility standards to the toolsets in Acrobat, will have you quickly “walking the tags tree” with confidence.

As Dax guides you through the basics of PDF accessibility, you’ll learn:

- Accessibility standards and how they differ: PDF/UA, Section 508, AODA, and more
- How to navigate the Acrobat accessibility toolsets
- Understanding basic tag structure: How to “walk the tags tree” and what that even means
- Differences between the Content, Tags, and Order panels

### Digitalizing Contracts Using Acrobat and Adobe Sign

![](/blog/adobe-max-2021-your-guide-to-document-cloud-sessions/06.png)

Session ID: S945Times: Thu, Oct 28th 12:00 AM — 12:30 AM PST

In this session, Hisako Okura, Instructor and Generalist at Adobe, will walk through how you can start digitizing contracts using Acrobat and Adobe Sign. Using company stamps or personal seals for signing contracts is inefficient for creative work. With Acrobat DC and Adobe Sign, you can exchange legally valid contracts more simply and easily. This session is a must for organizations looking to digitalize their contract work.
